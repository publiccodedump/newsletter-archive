The RevOps AI Brief — Issue #1

Welcome to the first issue of The RevOps AI Brief — a practitioner's filter on AI in revenue operations, minus the vendor hype.

Less landscape, more "show me the actual build." So this issue is four AI automations a RevOps IC can ship in a week (plus a 15-minute bonus) — each with the exact stack, the steps, and the catch that bites people. A note on sources and numbers at the very bottom.

 

Build 1 — A Clay table that scores ICP fit and feeds your sequencer

Stack: Clay → Apollo/Clearbit + LinkedIn + website scrape → HubSpot/Salesforce → Outreach/Salesloft

The build:
Seed a Clay table with company domain + contact name.
Add enrichment columns: Find company on LinkedIn (size, industry), Apollo/Clearbit (title, seniority, headcount, tech stack), and Scrape website (positioning, pricing model).
Add one AI column with a tight prompt: "You are a RevOps analyst. From the scraped site, LinkedIn description, industry and headcount, return: B2B SaaS? mid-market or enterprise? outbound motion y/n? and a 0–100 ICP score."
Layer a simple scoring formula: +20 B2B SaaS, +20 for 50–500 employees, +20 for a RevOps/Sales-Ops title, +20 for Salesforce/HubSpot in the stack, +20 for an outbound motion.
Push anything ≥ 70 to the CRM with the score + a generated two-sentence opener, and auto-enroll in a sequence.

The catch: the AI opener is where this breaks — instruct it to reference only scraped facts and never invent specifics, or you'll send confidently-wrong personalization at scale. Practitioners commonly report research time dropping from ~2–3 hours per 100 leads to ~15 minutes, and reply rates moving from ~1–2% to ~4–7% — directional, not gospel. Measure your own baseline before you celebrate.

Build 2 — AI lead routing in n8n (with a human-readable 'why')

Stack: n8n → HubSpot/Salesforce trigger → Clearbit/Apollo → LLM node (Claude/OpenAI) → Slack

The build:
Trigger on new contact/lead.
Enrich with Clearbit/Apollo (size, industry, title, seniority).
AI node: "Given title, company size, industry and our ICP, classify Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Non-ICP and explain in one sentence why."
Route on the result: Tier 1 + NA → owner A; Tier 1 + EMEA → owner B; Tier 2 → SDR pool; Non-ICP → nurture lifecycle.
Write `ICP_Tier` and `AI_Qualification_Notes` back to the CRM; post Tier 1s into a #hot-leads Slack channel with the context.

The catch: keep your deterministic rules (region, round-robin) as the backstop and let AI handle only the fuzzy tiering — a model misfire should never silently misroute revenue. Store the one-sentence rationale so you can audit why a lead got its tier. Teams report first-response time on hot leads falling from hours to under ~10 minutes, and ~30–60 min/day of manual routing reclaimed.

Build 3 — AI meeting prep + structured call summaries (your forecast's best friend)

Stack: Make or Zapier → CRM + Calendar → LLM → CRM notes / Notion

The build (two halves):
Pre-call: on a new calendar event with a CRM contact, pull the contact, company and last ~10 activities; have the LLM write a one-page brief — company + ICP-fit summary, known CRM context, 3–5 tailored discovery questions, 3 value hypotheses — into the CRM note.
Post-call: when the rep logs notes, have the LLM restructure them into fixed fields — pain points, decision criteria, stakeholders, timeline, next steps — and write those back.

The catch: the win isn't speed, it's structured data. Consistent next-steps and decision-criteria fields are exactly what make a forecast model (and the deal-scoring in Build 1) trustworthy. But reps must verify the summary — an LLM that quietly drops a stakeholder corrupts the very data you're trying to clean. This is the unglamorous build that makes everything else work.

Build 4 — Trigger plays: catch accounts when they're actually in-market

Stack: Clay (job + news/funding enrichment) → AI classifier → CRM → Make/Zapier scheduler

The build:
Seed a table with your target accounts.
Add Clay columns that scrape new job posts (filter to RevOps / Sales Ops / Salesforce Admin titles) and recent funding/news.
AI column: "Classify urgency low/medium/high from new RevOps hiring + funding in the last 90 days."
Filter to high, push to the CRM with `Trigger type`, `Trigger date` and an AI context summary, and create tasks for the owner.
Use Make/Zapier to re-run it nightly so the list stays live.

The catch: triggers create timing relevance, not fit — gate them behind your ICP score from Build 1, or you'll fast-route noise to your AEs. Practitioners report trigger-based outreach booking roughly 2–3x the meeting rate of bulk ICP blasts, precisely because the timing is right.

Sources: [3] [4]

 

One more (15 minutes) — and where this is all heading

The 15-minute version: a Zapier SLA nudge. On a new lead, wait 30–45 minutes; if `Last_Activity` is still empty, have an LLM draft a short Slack DM to the owner ("this Tier-1 lead hasn't been touched in 45 min — try this opener…"). Teams report touch-within-SLA rates climbing from ~60–70% to ~85–95%.

The bigger picture: notice what every build above shares — the AI does the drafting and classifying; you own the rules, the guardrails, and the call on when to trust it. That's the shift Forrester flags: RevOps is moving from delivering certainty to delivering probabilistic judgment. The ICs who win aren't the ones who automate the most — they're the ones who decide what's safe to automate and instrument the feedback loop to keep it honest.
